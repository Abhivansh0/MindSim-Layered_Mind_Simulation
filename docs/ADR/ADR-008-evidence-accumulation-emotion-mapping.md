# ADR-008: Evidence Accumulation Emotion Mapping

**Status:** Accepted

---

## Context

ADR-004 introduced range-based emotion profiles using membership functions.

Although the architecture solved several shortcomings of earlier approaches, it introduced a fundamental issue.

Emotion generation depended on predefined activation ranges and hard boundaries.

This conflicted with the continuous-state philosophy of MindSim.

Additionally, neuron lingering and accumulation naturally produce intermediate activation values during decay, making hard range boundaries increasingly incompatible with the neuron layer.

Over time, development effort shifted toward maintaining ranges rather than improving psychological realism.

Common issues included:

- HIGH range calibration
- Membership function adjustments
- Missing cluster penalties
- Contradictory penalties

These limitations motivated a redesign of the emotion layer.

---

## Decision

Replace range-based emotion profiles with an **evidence accumulation architecture**.

Each emotion is now defined using three semantic components:

- `core`
- `supporting`
- `contradicting`

Emotions are generated by accumulating evidence rather than matching predefined activation patterns.

The `core` appraisal is treated as psychologically important but is not a hard requirement.

Contradicting appraisals continuously suppress emotions rather than completely blocking them.

This architecture intentionally avoids hard thresholds and favors gradual emotional emergence.

Implementation details are documented in:

`docs/emotion_layer.md`

---

## Consequences

### Benefits

- Fully continuous emotion generation.
- Compatible with neuron lingering and accumulation.
- Removes dependence on arbitrary range boundaries.
- Contradictory appraisals naturally suppress emotions.
- Emotion definitions become psychologically interpretable.
- Schemas become easier to tune than mathematical formulas.
- Reduces architectural complexity.

### Tradeoffs

- Emotion quality now depends heavily on schema design.
- Poorly designed schemas can cause excessive co-activation.
- Validation shifts from mathematical calibration to psychological evaluation.

---

## Philosophical Shift

The emotion layer no longer asks:

> "What does fear look like?"

Instead, it asks:

> "What evidence exists for fear?"

Emotions are treated as emergent states that arise from accumulated appraisal evidence rather than pattern matches against predefined profiles.

This moves MindSim from **pattern matching** toward **evidence accumulation**.
